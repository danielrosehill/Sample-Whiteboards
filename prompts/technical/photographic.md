## Prompt

> Take this whiteboard photograph and convert it into a photorealistic 3D-rendered diagram. Remove the physical whiteboard, markers, frame, and any background elements -- output the diagram on a clean surface like a light wooden desk or frosted glass table, shot from directly above. Correct any perspective distortion so the output appears as a perfectly straight-on, top-down view. Preserve all the original content, text, and diagrams. When reading handwritten text, infer the correct spelling of technical terms, product names, and proper nouns rather than transcribing handwriting literally (e.g. "Proxmox" not "Proxknox", "Kubernetes" not "Kubernites"). Render each box, container, or concept as a physical object -- glossy acrylic blocks, frosted glass cards, or brushed metal plates with realistic reflections and soft shadows. Arrows and connectors should look like thin metal rods or illuminated fiber-optic strips. Text should appear etched, printed, or embossed on the surfaces. Use studio-quality lighting with soft diffusion. The result should look like a product photograph of a physical scale model of the diagram -- tangible, premium, and strikingly real.

## Style Notes

- Think: product photography, 3D render showcase, physical prototype model.
- Realistic materials: glass, metal, acrylic with proper reflections and shadows.
- Studio lighting with soft, even diffusion.
- Makes abstract concepts feel tangible and physical.
